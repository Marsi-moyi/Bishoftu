Hora Arsade Lake

It is the oldest lake, nearest to the center and it is name is derived from the name of a person which called Harsadi from the Liban ethnic group. On this lake Irrecha Malka ceremony is conducted once a year. It is the largest of other lakes around Bishoftu and there were 36 bird species. The Oromo culture ceremony Irrecha (Thanks giving ceremony) conducted once a year a million of local and foreign visitors participate and it is an apportunity for a colorful ceremony. On top of this cultural event becomes one of the greatest attraction sites for Tourists.

Cheleleka Lake

It is one of the shallow sesonal lake that formed by the rain water and non-crater with many kinds of species, around 106 bird species are existed. There is no so much satisfied tourist flow continuously, because it is a seasonal and formed by rain water during summer season only. Communities are using for local irrigation and harvesting from this lake. This lake is currently becoming dry, because of water pollution due to flood sewerage from the partial part of the city. A fleeting seasonal lake that only forms during the rainy season (July-August) and slowly dries as the sun re-emerges from February onward.

Lake Bishoftu

Lake Bishoftu, which lies immediately south of the town centre, is the closest to the rest of the town. The lake is accessible via a steep cattle path, and is often frequented by tourists and locals alike, particularly during the warm summer months, where they have been known to swim within its water.The best way to appreciate this scenic body of water is from the crater rim at one of the hotels, drink in hand. The view is sensational. According to local lore, this, the second-deepest lake in Ethiopia, is home to a sleeping devil. From time to time his evil gases kill the fish and send them bobbing to the surface to be scooped up by delighted waterbirds.

Kilole Lake

The lake is located on the eastern of the city. The lake is surrounded by farm land and heavy cave mountains. It is an attractive place as it is a natural lake as well as a wonderful mountain covered with forests and caves which act as a shelter for different species of birds has been serving. It is 12 km east from center of the city. However, the lake is not as developed as other lakes and is not visited by local and foreign tourists. Since the half of the lake is surrounded only by agricultural land and it would not developed for tourists like other lakes.

Lake Green(Hadho)

The lake is located 10 km southwest of the city behind the Ethiopian Air Force base. The lake looks green because of its algae. Hills nearby is an even more spectacular, colorful and beautiful when they viewed from the nearby Lake Megerisa. The lake is a tranquil and it has no development activities around. The water source of the lake is a natural source and the water is alkaline, and is believed to nest in the rocky hills surrounding the lake. There is an artificial lake opening at the far end of the lake, which allows cattle to get water to the lake shore. Currently the lake does not have the opportunity for development and attention will be needed the for tourist market.
